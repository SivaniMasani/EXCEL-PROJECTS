##  Project 1 -  Customer Transaction Analysis

###  Business Problem
A retail store wants to understand customer purchasing behaviour,
identify top-performing sales channels, and analyse revenue trends
across job titles and time periods to improve business strategy.

###  Dataset
- **Period:** December 2023 — February 2024
- **Total Transactions:** 443
- **Total Revenue:** £213,550
- **Purchase Channels:** Website, App, In Store, Phone

---

###  Key Findings

| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
|  Total Revenue | **£213,550** |
|  Top Purchase Channel | **Website - £81,070 (182 transactions)** |
|  Highest Success Rate | **In Store - 76%** |
|  Top Spending Job Title | **Professor - £35,225** |
|  Best Month | **December 2023 - £83,605** |
|  Revenue Trend | **Declining - Dec £83K to Feb £58K** |
|  Lowest Channel | **Phone - £14,575 (26 transactions)** |

---

###  Business Insights

**1. Website dominates sales**
Website transactions generate the highest revenue at £81,070 
suggesting the business should invest more in its online presence
and digital marketing.

**2. Revenue is declining month on month**
December generated £83,605 but dropped to £58,270 in February 
a 30% decline. This signals a need to investigate post-holiday
customer retention strategies.

**3. Professors are the highest spending customer segment**
At £35,225 total spend, professors represent a high-value customer
segment worth targeting with loyalty programmes or premium offers.

**4. Phone channel is underperforming**
With only 26 transactions and £14,575 revenue, the phone channel
may not be worth continued investment compared to digital channels.

##  Project 2 -  Electric Vehicle Population Analysis

###  Business Problem
Analyse electric vehicle registration data across the USA to
understand EV adoption trends, manufacturer dominance, and
electric range performance across different makes and models.

### Key Findings

| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
|  Total Vehicles Analysed | **150,482** |
| Top Manufacturer | **Tesla - 68,983 vehicles (46%)** |
|  BEV vs PHEV Split | **BEV 78% vs PHEV 22%** |
|  Highest Avg Electric Range | **Jaguar - 204 miles** |
|  Tesla Avg Range | **90 miles** |
|  Second Manufacturer | **Nissan - 13,497 vehicles** |
|  Third Manufacturer | **Chevrolet - 12,026 vehicles** |

###  Business Insights

**1. Tesla completely dominates the EV market**
With 68,983 registrations, Tesla accounts for nearly half of
all EVs - more than Nissan and Chevrolet combined.

**2. BEV is the clear winner over PHEV**
78% of vehicles are fully electric (BEV) vs 22% hybrid (PHEV)
showing strong consumer preference for fully electric vehicles.

**3. Jaguar leads on range despite low volume**
Jaguar has the highest average electric range at 204 miles 
significantly ahead of Tesla's 90 miles — despite having far
fewer vehicles registered.

**4. EV adoption is accelerating**
Vehicle registrations peaked in 2023 showing rapid year on year
growth in EV adoption across the USA.

###  Excel Skills Used
- Pivot Tables - vehicle count by make and type
- Average calculations - electric range by manufacturer
- Data sorting and ranking
- Large dataset handling (150,000+ rows)
